Carstensz Pyramid — Peak Overview

  • Height: 4,884 m (16,024 ft)
  • Range: Sudirman Range
  • Location: Papua, Indonesia
  • Coordinates: 4.0833° S, 137.1833° E
  • First Ascent: 1962
  • First Climbers: Heinrich Harrer & Team

Carstensz Pyramid, also known as Puncak Jaya, is the highest peak of Oceania and the only technical rock-climbing summit among the Seven Summits. Its sharp limestone ridges rise dramatically from dense equatorial jungle.

Geography Papua

Geography & Location

Carstensz rises from the misty rainforests of Papua, standing as a jagged limestone tower. Its glaciers (now rapidly melting) once formed the only equatorial icefields in the region.

History First Ascent: 1962

Climbing History & Expeditions

The first ascent was made by Austrian mountaineer Heinrich Harrer. For decades, access has been limited due to political and tribal conflict, making Carstensz one of the hardest Seven Summits to reach logistically.

Hazards Rock • Rain

Challenges & Dangers

Carstensz Pyramid involves steep rock climbing, fixed ropes, slippery limestone, constant rain, and long exposed traverses. Jungle approaches are extremely remote and weather changes rapidly.

Culture Papua Tribes

Cultural Significance & Conservation

The mountain lies in an area rich with Papua tribal cultures. Carstensz is also part of a fragile rainforest ecosystem affected by mining operations and climate-driven glacial retreat.

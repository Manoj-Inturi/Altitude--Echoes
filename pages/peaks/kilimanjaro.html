Kilimanjaro — Peak Overview

  • Height: 5,895 m (19,341 ft)
  • Range: Eastern Rift Mountains
  • Location: Tanzania
  • Coordinates: 3.0674° S, 37.3556° E
  • First Ascent: 1889
  • First Climbers: Hans Meyer & Ludwig Purtscheller

Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest mountain, is a massive freestanding volcano rising above the savannas of Tanzania. Known for its iconic snowcap, lush ecological zones, and the world-famous “Roof of Africa,” Kilimanjaro is one of the most climbed high-altitude peaks globally.

Geography Tanzania

Geography & Location

Kilimanjaro consists of three volcanic cones — Kibo, Mawenzi, and Shira. The mountain features rainforest, alpine desert, and arctic summit zones, making it one of the most diverse ecological ascents in the world.

History First Ascent: 1889

Climbing History & Expeditions

The first recorded ascent was made in 1889 by Hans Meyer and Ludwig Purtscheller. Multiple trekking routes exist today — including Machame, Marangu, Lemosho, and Rongai — attracting adventurers from around the world to experience high-altitude trekking.

Hazards AMS • Weather

Challenges & Dangers

Although non-technical, Kilimanjaro poses risks such as altitude sickness, rapid weather changes, freezing summit temperatures, and steep final ascents. Proper acclimatization is essential.

Culture Chagga Region

Cultural Significance & Conservation

Kilimanjaro holds cultural and spiritual meaning for the Chagga people, who live on its fertile lower slopes. Kilimanjaro National Park protects the mountain’s ecosystems, wildlife, and glaciers — which are rapidly shrinking due to climate change.

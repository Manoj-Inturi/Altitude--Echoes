Peak Overview

Gasherbrum II — Peak Overview

  • Height: 8,035 m (26,362 ft)
  • Range: Karakoram
  • Location: Pakistan–China Border
  • Coordinates: 35.7580° N, 76.6530° E
  • First Ascent: 7 July 1956
  • First Climbers: Fritz Moravec, Josef Larch & Hans Willenpart (Austria)

Gasherbrum II, sometimes called the “Easy Eight-Thousander,” is the 13th-highest mountain in the world. Although considered one of the more accessible 8000ers, it still presents major challenges, including harsh weather, large crevasses, and steep summit slopes.

Geography Karakoram

Geography & Location

Gasherbrum II rises from the confluence of the Baltoro and South Gasherbrum Glaciers, forming part of a dense cluster of towering ice peaks. Its pyramid-like summit stands out against the rugged Karakoram skyline.

History First Ascent: 1956

Climbing History & Expeditions

The first ascent of G2 was made by an Austrian expedition in 1956. Since then it has become a popular objective for mountaineers attempting their first 8000-meter peak, thanks to its relatively moderate climbing difficulties.

Hazards Crevasses • Snow Ridges

Challenges & Dangers

While technically less steep than other 8000ers, Gasherbrum II features large crevasse fields, unstable snow bridges, avalanche-prone slopes, and powerful winds sweeping through the Karakoram.

Culture Gilgit-Baltistan

Cultural Significance & Conservation

Gasherbrum II lies in Pakistan’s remote Gilgit-Baltistan region. The mountain is part of the Central Karakoram National Park, a protected zone of glaciers, rare wildlife, and high-altitude ecosystems that local communities depend on for survival.

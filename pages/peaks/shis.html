Shishapangma
Peak Overview

Shishapangma — Peak Overview

  • Height: 8,027 m (26,335 ft)
  • Range: Himalayas
  • Location: Tibet, China
  • Coordinates: 28.3525° N, 85.7797° E
  • First Ascent: 2 May 1964
  • First Climbers: Xu Jing & Chinese Expedition Team

Shishapangma, the lowest of the 8000ers, is revered for its elegant slopes and sweeping snowfields. Although considered less technical, its weather, avalanche risk, and high altitude still pose serious challenges to climbers.

Geography Tibet

Geography & Location

Shishapangma’s broad west face and gentle snow ridges dominate the surrounding Tibetan plateau. Its isolated position makes it one of the most visually distinctive peaks in the Himalaya, rising abruptly from vast high-altitude plains.

History First Ascent: 1964

Climbing History & Expeditions

Shishapangma was the last 8000er to be climbed, owing to restrictions in Tibet. The 1964 Chinese expedition made the historic first ascent via the northwest face, opening the mountain to later international teams in the decades that followed.

Hazards Snowfields • Avalanches

Challenges & Dangers

Long, exposed snow slopes make Shishapangma particularly prone to slab avalanches. Weather can shift rapidly above 7,500 meters, and the true summit requires traversing a narrow, unstable ridge.

Culture Tibet

Cultural Significance & Conservation

In Tibetan culture, Shishapangma is known as “Gosainthan,” meaning “The Grasslands Above the Windows of Heaven.” The region carries spiritual importance and falls within sensitive, protected zones that preserve Tibetan wildlife and mountain ecosystems.

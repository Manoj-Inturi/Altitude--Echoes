Peak Overview

Gasherbrum I — Peak Overview

  • Height: 8,080 m (26,509 ft)
  • Range: Karakoram
  • Location: Pakistan–China Border
  • Coordinates: 35.7206° N, 76.6965° E
  • First Ascent: 5 July 1958
  • First Climbers: Pete Schoening & Andy Kauffman (USA)

Gasherbrum I, also known as Hidden Peak or K5, is the 11th-highest mountain in the world. Its towering ice walls, dramatic ridges, and remote location in the heart of the Karakoram make it one of the most isolated and pristine 8000ers.

Geography Karakoram

Geography & Location

Gasherbrum I stands deep within the Karakoram range, near the Baltoro Glacier, surrounded by some of the highest and most rugged peaks on Earth. Its name, meaning “Beautiful Mountain,” reflects the massive ice faces and sharp ridges that define its unique structure.

History First Ascent: 1958

Climbing History & Expeditions

After several reconnaissance attempts in the early 20th century, a strong American expedition finally reached the summit in 1958. Since then, climbers have attempted various technical routes, but the peak remains less frequently climbed due to its remoteness.

Hazards Avalanches • Ice Walls

Challenges & Dangers

Gasherbrum I features towering seracs, steep ice ramps, and complex crevasse systems. Harsh winds, extreme cold, and difficult glacier navigation make it one of the more technically demanding 8000ers despite its modest elevation relative to others.

Culture Gilgit-Baltistan

Cultural Significance & Conservation

Gasherbrum I lies in a remote region of Gilgit-Baltistan, where traditional Balti culture thrives along ancient trade routes. The region is part of the Central Karakoram National Park, which protects glaciers, wildlife, and fragile alpine environments.

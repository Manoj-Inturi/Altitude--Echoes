Peak Overview

Broad Peak — Peak Overview

  • Height: 8,051 m (26,414 ft)
  • Range: Karakoram
  • Location: Pakistan–China Border
  • Coordinates: 35.8136° N, 76.5650° E
  • First Ascent: 9 June 1957
  • First Climbers: Fritz Wintersteller, Marcus Schmuck, Kurt Diemberger & Hermann Buhl

Broad Peak earns its name from its long, wide summit ridge stretching over 1.5 km. Located near K2, it offers a mix of challenging snow slopes and exposed ridge climbing, making it a popular but serious objective for experienced mountaineers.

Geography Karakoram

Geography & Location

Broad Peak rises above the Baltoro Glacier, forming part of the Gasherbrum massif. Its long ridgeline connects several summits, with dramatic snow slopes descending toward the glacier systems that dominate the Karakoram.

History First Ascent: 1957

Climbing History & Expeditions

The first ascent of Broad Peak was achieved in alpine style without fixed ropes, an impressive feat for the era. The Austrian team tackled the west ridge and upper slopes, establishing a route that remains the standard climb today.

Hazards Exposure • Avalanches

Challenges & Dangers

Despite being one of the “easier” 8000ers, Broad Peak features long, exposed ridge sections, unstable snowfields, and harsh winds funnelling through the Karakoram peaks. The extended summit ridge can be especially dangerous in poor weather.

Culture Gilgit-Baltistan

Cultural Significance & Conservation

Broad Peak is located within Pakistan’s Gilgit-Baltistan region, home to Balti communities with a rich history of high-altitude life. The area forms part of the Central Karakoram National Park, a crucial zone for glacier conservation.

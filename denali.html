Peak Overview

Denali — Peak Overview

  • Height: 6,190 m (20,310 ft)
  • Range: Alaska Range
  • Location: Alaska, USA
  • Coordinates: 63.0695° N, 151.0074° W
  • First Ascent: 1913
  • First Climbers: Hudson Stuck, Walter Harper, Harry Karstens, Robert Tatum

Denali, the highest mountain in North America, rises dramatically from the Alaskan wilderness. Known for its brutal cold, massive vertical relief, and unpredictable storms, Denali remains one of the most physically demanding climbs on the Seven Summits.

Geography Alaska

Geography & Location

Denali dominates the Alaska Range with the largest base-to-summit elevation gain of any mountain on Earth. Surrounding glaciers, deep valleys, and subarctic landscapes create one of the most dramatic mountain environments in the world.

History First Ascent: 1913

Climbing History & Expeditions

The first successful ascent was achieved in 1913 by an expedition led by Hudson Stuck. Modern expeditions usually follow the West Buttress Route, established in 1951, but Denali’s extreme conditions ensure every ascent remains a serious undertaking.

Hazards Cold • Crevasses

Challenges & Dangers

Denali is infamous for severe cold (-40°C and below), violent storms, crevasse fields, heavy loads carried by climbers, and the high latitude that lowers atmospheric pressure, making altitude effects even stronger.

Culture Athabascan Region

Cultural Significance & Conservation

Known as “Denali,” meaning “The High One” in the Koyukon Athabascan language, the mountain has deep spiritual significance for Indigenous communities. Today it stands at the heart of Denali National Park, a protected wilderness rich in wildlife and Arctic ecosystems.
